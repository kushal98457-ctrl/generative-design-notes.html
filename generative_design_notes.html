Generative Design & Topology Optimization – Clean Notes

Generative Design

Generative design is a simulation tool used to generate multiple design alternatives based on the designer's input.

Flow of Generative Design

Designer's input
↓
GD software
↓
Multiple design alternatives

The software explores all possible permutations and generates multiple alternatives.

Hence, generative design is a design exploration process.

Working of Generative Design

During generative design, the computer weighs and designs hundreds and thousands of design alternatives.

From these alternatives, the engineer selects the best design that meets the important requirements.

Generative design combines human creativity and machine learning to develop a product.

It relies on the power of cloud computing and machine learning to create multiple designs and better products.

Cloud Computing (Note)

Cloud computing is a service through the internet to access:

• Data storage
• Servers
• Databases
• Networking
• Software

Machine Learning

AI learns from each iteration.

It identifies:

• Which works
• Which does not work
• Helps in developing a better product

What Generative Design Does Other Than Part Creation

Evaluates different manufacturing methods to create a part.

Provides:

• Cost implications of different manufacturing methods
• Labor and safety considerations

Considers weight of the component.

Advantages of Generative Design

• Accelerates product development progress
• Takes less time to create a part
• Improves part performance
• Produces lightweight (optimized) designs
• Sustainable / eco‑friendly
• Enables part consolidation (multiple components combined into a single component)

How to Create Generative Design

The designer should convey design requirements to the software by:

• Creating a basic design
• Defining where the load is acting on the design
• Defining where the constraints act

Designer should assign special constraints to the part generation.

Preserve Geometry

Bodies assigned as preserve geometry are excluded from generative modification.

It retains the geometry of a component and incorporates it into the final shape of the design.

Preserved geometry is displayed in green colour.

Rules for Applying Preserve Geometry

• Load‑acting geometry is mandatory to preserve.
• Constraint geometry may or may not be preserved.

Obstacle Geometry

Assigned to represent spaces that must be avoided in the design.

Steps to Apply Obstacle Geometry

• Create a body representing the space to avoid
• Assign it as obstacle geometry

Obstacle geometry is displayed in red colour.

Spatial Constraints – Start Shape

Start shape prevents material formation outside the boundary.

It influences the shape of the generative design.

Displayed in yellow colour.

Start shape is optional.

Not using a start shape results in more variation in outcomes.

Rules for Start Shape

• Can be assigned to only one body
• Must be in contact with all preserve geometry

Topology Optimization

Topology optimization is a process of reducing the weight of a component by carefully removing unnecessary material without sacrificing performance.

A topology‑optimized part should be lighter in weight and retain strength.

Topology

Study of surface, shape, or geometrical properties.

Optimization

Achieving the best result by using resources effectively.

Topology Optimization – Explanation

It is the process of creating the best design using minimum material.

Creation of Topology Optimized Designs

Achieved by redistribution of material (material allocation) according to the load‑carrying path.

Load Path

Load path varies based on the direction and magnitude of the load.

Part Failure

In topology optimization, part failure occurs if material is removed from the load path.

Topology Optimization Simulation in Autodesk Fusion 360

Autodesk Fusion 360 includes topology optimization simulation.

Simulation is based on Finite Element Analysis (FEA).

Simulation considers:

• Load
• Constraints
• Materials
• Direction and size of external forces

How Topology Optimization Simulation Works

The simulation process relies heavily on Finite Element Analysis (FEA / FEM).

Based on FEA, it identifies:

• High stress regions
• Low or no stress regions

Material is removed from the least stressed regions.

Benefits of Topology Optimization

• Reduces production time
• Improves strength‑to‑weight ratio
• Uses the least material possible

Advantages of Topology Optimization in Automobiles

• Reduces fuel consumption
• Reduces carbon emissions
• Improves acceleration and tyre performance
• Improves braking
• Reduces wear and tear of brakes

Constraints

Constraints prevent the part from moving under applied loads.

Constraints transfer the load to other parts or bodies.

Preserved Region

Preserved region hides selected regions of a model from shape optimization.

Any material within the preserved region remains intact.

A green cylinder appears around the selected region indicating preserved region.

Mesh Generation

Mesh generation is the process of converting continuous geometry into discrete geometry.

Creating mesh is important for simulation analysis.

Finer mesh → more accurate results.
